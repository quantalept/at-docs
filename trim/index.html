    ✂️ TRIM

    📖 Trim Description

    Trim refers to the process of cutting out unwanted segments from the beginning, end, or even middle of an audio track. It’s a fundamental audio editing technique used to eliminate silence, errors, noise, or irrelevant sections.

    Trimming ensures a clean, focused, and professional-sounding result. It’s commonly used in music production, podcasts, voiceovers, interviews, and all audio-based content to enhance clarity and flow.

    🎚️ Trim – User Guide

    Steps:

    1. Select the audio file you want to edit.
    2. Once loaded, the waveform of the audio will appear with trim handles (markers) at both ends.
    3. Drag the handles to highlight the part of the audio you want to keep.
    4. If you want to remove a specific middle section, select that region and click the cut icon below the waveform — this will delete the selected part.
    5. For precision editing, use the Start and End time fields to enter exact trimming values.
    6. Click the Save button at the top right of the screen once you're satisfied with your trim.
    7. Choose your output format (e.g., MP3, WAV).
    8. Optionally, enter a new title for the edited file.
    9. Finally, click the Save button at the bottom to export your trimmed audio to local storage.

    ✅ Tip: Always preview your selection before cutting or saving to avoid accidentally removing important audio.
